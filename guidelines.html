PROCOMM ‘24
GENERAL GUIDELINES

Round-1: Online Abstract Presentation

  • Participants can compete individually or as a group with a maximum of 4 members.
  • Teams can work on any project that adheres to the given problem statement.
  • The submitted idea proposals should have tangible outcomes.
  • Each team can submit only a single abstract.
  • The abstract must be in pdf format.
  • The team lead must be an IEEE ComSoc member.
  • Multiple submissions will not be entertained.
  • Participants are advised to make a copy of the given submission format and fill in the necessary details.
  • Strictly follow the rules and format regarding the submission of the abstract.
  • In case of any disputes or concerns, the decision of the organizers and judging panel will be final.

Round-2: Offline Hackathon

  • Round 2 will take place in offline mode.
  • A detailed list of hardware components will be provided to the shortlisted teams. Should they require any additional hardware, we recommend bringing it with them.
  • The use of pre-existing code or projects is not allowed, except for open-source libraries or frameworks.
  • All code developed during the Hackathon should be the original work of the team. Participants cannot use code or assets created by someone else without proper permissions or licenses.
  • Participants must adhere to ethical standards and abide by the rules and regulations set by the organizers. Any form of cheating, plagiarism, or unfair practices will result in immediate disqualification.
  • The result decided upon by the judges will be final. It may include aspects such as innovation, technical implementation, user experience, scalability, and relevance to the theme.
  • Participants should adhere to a code of conduct that promotes inclusivity, respect, and professionalism. Any form of harassment, discrimination, or inappropriate behavior will not be tolerated.
  • In case of any disputes or concerns, the decision of the organizers and judging panel will be final.
  • The organizers and sponsors of the Hackathon hold no liability for any damages, losses, or injuries incurred during the event. Participants are responsible for their safety, equipment, and actions.
  • Organizers reserve the right to make changes to the Hackathon rules, format, or prizes at any time. Any modifications will be communicated to the participants in a clear and timely manner.
  • There will be three (First, Second, and Third) prizes. The decision of the evaluation panel members based on the performance of the team will be final.
